Most of the other courses provide a few easy homework. You solve them and everyone is happy! But the truth is you haven't learned well!

The homework in the first half of the course will always range from easy to hard. Most of the time you can't solve the medium-to-hard in the first go!

  • This is expected and normal.

  • Marking hard problems as ToDos and coming back later is an effective technique to gain a skill

  • Consider the following 2 approaches while tackling the homework

Easy Problems First Approach

  • Tackle the easy problems and skip the medium/hard problems

  • After The Functions Section, come back and tackle the medium problems

  • After the Educational System Project (or more later), come back and tackle the ToDos and hard problems.

  • Keep iterating on your ToDos


Challenge All Approach

  • Always give a trial for the whole homework.

  • Try 30-60 minutes per problems, then check out my solutions

    • Feel free to go occasionally beyond that or even always, but you will be a bit slower. Trade-off.

    • Never check out the solutions without real trial with the problem

  • Don't feel bad if you can't get or solve it (by yourself or even after checking out my solutions).

  • Whatever hard, mark as ToDos and come back later

  • Keep iterating on your ToDos
